🧠 Mental Health Text Classifier

Welcome to my final-year project.

Mental health conditions such as anxiety and depression are increasingly important topics, particularly following the rise in cases observed since COVID-19. This work is inspired by the concept of digital phenotyping, where behavioural and linguistic data from digital interactions can provide insight into psychological wellbeing.

This project explores how natural language processing can be used to detect patterns in text that may be associated with mental health signals. The goal is not diagnosis, but to demonstrate how language patterns can reflect emotional states.

Enter a piece of text (for example, a social media post), and the model will analyse the language and return a predicted category along with a confidence score.

🧠 About the Model

This system uses a fine-tuned transformer-based language model trained on labelled mental health-related text data.

The model processes input text through tokenisation, embedding layers, and contextual attention mechanisms to identify linguistic patterns associated with anxiety, depression, or neutral sentiment.

It outputs a probability distribution across classes, and the final prediction is based on the highest probability score.

⚠️ IMPORTANT DISCLAIMER: This tool is for educational and research purposes only. It is NOT a medical or diagnostic system.
